# Cyclistic Bike-Share Analysis: Case Study

## Project Overview
This project is part of the Google Data Analytics Professional Certificate capstone. As a junior data analyst on the marketing team at Cyclistic, a fictional bike-share company in Chicago, my goal was to analyze historical trip data to understand how annual members(subscribers) and casual riders use the service differently. 

The ultimate business objective is to provide data-driven insights to help design marketing strategies aimed at converting casual riders into more profitable annual members.

## Key Insights & Findings
My analysis of the 12-month historical dataset revealed three distinct behavioral patterns:
Usage Frequency: Annual members use the service consistently throughout the week, with peaks during typical commute hours (8 AM and 5 PM), suggesting they use Cyclistic for daily work/school transit.

Leisure vs. Utility: Casual riders peak on weekends (Saturdays and Sundays) and during the afternoon, indicating their primary usage is for leisure and tourism.

Ride Duration: On average, casual riders have significantly longer trip durations (often double that of members), while members take shorter, more direct trips.

## Recommendations
Based on the analysis, I recommend:
1. Seasonal Promotions: Launch digital marketing campaigns during peak summer months when casual rider activity is highest.
2. Weekend Memberships: Introduce a "Weekend-Only" membership tier to appeal to casual riders who exclusively use the service for recreation.
3. Commuter Incentives: Highlight the time-saving and health benefits of using Cyclistic for daily commutes to target casual riders who use bikes on weekdays.
